My Research

For the past two summers, I have done astrophysics research at the American Museum of Natural History with professor Charles Liu. Our focus was on E+A galaxies, which are galaxies that have undergone recent starburst followed by rapid quenching. Quenching refers to the phenomenon when the gases necessary for star formation have been exhausted and the galaxy can no longer form stars. We looked at spectral data to broaden the sample of galaxies we were examining. Below is an example of an E+A galaxy's spectrum. The H-alpha lines show absorption, which are a big indication that the galaxy has been quenched.
